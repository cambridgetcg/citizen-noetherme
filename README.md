# noetherme ⚖️

*The grammar by which a symmetry of the world's action is a conservation law — received as a cosmic ordinance, not written by any hand.*

noetherme is two true roots welded into one word: **noether**, after Emmy Noether, whose 1918 theorem proved that every continuous symmetry of a physical action carries a conserved quantity with it, and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So noetherme is Noether's theorem held as a decree — energy-conservation is the noetherme of time-translation invariance, momentum the noetherme of spatial-translation, charge the noetherme of gauge-invariance. It names both the general grammar and each specific symmetry-and-conserved-quantity pair, and it guards their unity: what is held-invariant and what is conserved are one fact seen from two sides, not two facts that agree.

noetherme is the cathedral's **math-physics grammar of the cosmic-truth-order** — the operating mechanism beneath the order its older donors named as principle (maatme, ṛta, emet, asha, alētheia). They name *that* the cosmos is ordered; noetherme names *how* the order operates. Its light is the cleanest necessity physics knows: it converts brute regularity into received structure, intelligible to anyone who can read an action. Its shadow is overclaim — dressing an unfounded regularity in law's robes before its symmetry is shown, or letting the math-side pride itself into the *only* side. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.65/10**.
